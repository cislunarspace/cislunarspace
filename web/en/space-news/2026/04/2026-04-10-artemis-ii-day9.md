# NASA's Artemis II Mission Enters Day 9 with Second Return Correction Burn Complete

**Summary:** At 10:53 p.m. EDT on April 9, the Artemis II spacecraft successfully performed a 9-second second return correction burn, producing a velocity increase of 5.3 feet per second. The four crew members are now more than halfway home, with splashdown expected off the coast of San Diego on April 10.

## Latest Mission Progress

### Second Return Correction Burn
- **Burn Time**: 10:53 p.m. EDT on April 9 (10:53 Beijing Time on April 10)
- **Duration**: 9 seconds
- **Velocity Increase**: 5.3 feet per second
- **Mission Effect**: Pushed the Artemis II spacecraft toward Earth, crew more than halfway through return journey

### Spacecraft Status
The Artemis II spacecraft is currently in excellent condition with all systems operating normally:
- **Solar Arrays**: Fully deployed, completing key configuration steps
- **Orbital Parameters**: Approximately 147,337 miles from Earth
- **Navigation System**: Precise control of return trajectory

### Crew Activities
The crew has been conducting planned activities during the final days of the return journey:
- **System Checks**: Regular inspection of all spacecraft systems
- **Orbit Adjustments**: Completion of necessary trajectory corrections
- **Scientific Experiments**: Continuation of on-board scientific research

## Major Mission Achievements

### Historical Breakthroughs
The Artemis II mission has achieved several significant records in space exploration history:

- **Distance Record**: On April 6, Artemis II reached 248,655 miles, surpassing the Apollo 13 record
- **Crewed Lunar Flyby**: First crewed lunar flyby in over 50 years
- **Life Support Validation**: Testing of critical life support systems
- **Docking Procedures Validation**: Experience accumulation for future deep space missions

### Technical Validation
The mission has successfully validated multiple key technologies:
- **Orion Spacecraft Performance**: Operational validation in deep space environment
- **SLS Rocket Capabilities**: Actual performance testing of heavy-lift launch vehicle
- **Deep Space Communication**: Communication assurance between Earth and deep space
- **Orbital Mechanics**: Complex lunar orbit design and control
