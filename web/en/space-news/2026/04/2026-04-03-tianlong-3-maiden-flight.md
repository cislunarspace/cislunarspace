# Tianlong-3 Maiden Flight Success: China's First Falcon 9-Class Commercial Rocket Reaches Orbit

![Tianlong-3 Launch Vehicle (Space Pioneer official render)](http://www.spacepioneer.cc/_nuxt/img/NTL-3.2b1b695.png)
*Image credit: Space Pioneer*

**Summary:** On April 3, 2026 at 04:00 UTC, Space Pioneer's **Tianlong-3** liquid-fueled launch vehicle successfully completed its maiden flight from the **Jiuquan Satellite Launch Center**, deploying a batch of **Qianfan** constellation satellites into a **polar orbit**. As China's first commercial rocket with a low-Earth orbit capacity exceeding 17 tonnes — comparable to SpaceX's Falcon 9 — the Tianlong-3 marks a significant milestone in China's commercial space sector.

## Rocket Specifications

- **Height**: ~71 m
- **Diameter**: 3.8 m
- **Liftoff mass**: ~590 tonnes
- **Liftoff thrust**: ~855 tonnes (9 × TH-12 kerolox engines)
- **LEO capacity**: 17–22 tonnes
- **SSO capacity**: 10–14 tonnes
- **First stage engines**: 9 × Tianhuo-12 (TH-12), ~109 tf sea-level thrust each
- **Second stage engine**: 1 × TH-12V (vacuum-optimized)
- **Propellant**: LOX / RP-1 (kerolox)
- **Design reuse**: First stage up to 10–20 flights

## Mission Significance

Tianlong-3 is the next-generation medium-lift vehicle from Space Pioneer, following the successful Tianlong-2 maiden flight in April 2023 — the first orbital launch by a Chinese private liquid-fueled rocket. With its 9-engine first-stage configuration closely mirroring SpaceX's Falcon 9, Tianlong-3 aims to deliver:

- **36-satellite** multi-payload deployment capability (validated through transport and vibration tests in November 2025)
- **Reusability**: Autonomous vertical landing of the first stage, designed for at least 10 reuses
- **Low-cost high-cadence launch**: Supporting a target cadence of 60+ launches per year

The maiden flight carried **Qianfan** low-Earth orbit communication constellation satellites, part of a planned mega-constellation of over 10,000 satellites for China's satellite internet infrastructure.

## A Rocky Road to Launch

Development was not without setbacks. On June 30, 2024, a Tianlong-3 first stage accidentally detached from its test stand during a static fire in Gongyi due to a structural failure, crashing and exploding 1.5 km away with no casualties. Space Pioneer subsequently implemented **127 corrective measures**, including doubling the hold-down arms.

On September 15, 2025, the first stage successfully completed a ~30-second static fire on the HOS-1 sea launch platform at Haiyang Space Port, confirming full recovery. The successful maiden flight from Jiuquan validates the overall vehicle design.

## 2026: China's "Reusable Rocket Year"

Industry observers have dubbed 2026 China's **"Year of Reusability."** Alongside Tianlong-3, LandSpace's Zhuque-3, iSpace's Hyperbola-3, and Galaxy Energy's Zhishenxing-1 are all targeting maiden flights or recovery tests this year. Tianlong-3's successful debut fires the opening shot in this technology race to slash launch costs.
