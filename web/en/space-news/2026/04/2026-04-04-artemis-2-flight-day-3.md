# Artemis II Flight Day 3: Trajectory Correction Cancelled, Spacecraft on Precise Path to the Moon

**Summary:** As Artemis II enters its third day, NASA's Mission Control Center in Houston elected to cancel the spacecraft's first planned outbound trajectory correction burn. Orion's trajectory is already precise enough that the adjustment is unnecessary. The four-person crew — NASA astronauts Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and CSA astronaut Jeremy Hansen — are steadily en route to the Moon, with the lunar flyby expected on April 6 (EDT).

![View from Orion during the Artemis II mission](./figures/2026-04-04-artemis-2-flight-day-3/hero.jpg)
*Credit: NASA*

## Mission Progress

On Flight Day 2 (April 2), Orion successfully completed the translunar injection (TLI) burn, firing its main engine for approximately 5 minutes and 50 seconds. This officially sent the crew on a trajectory toward the Moon — marking the first time humans have left Earth orbit since the Apollo program in 1972.

On Flight Day 3, the crew began their second full day in space after an approximately eight-hour rest period, settling into the rhythm of deep-space operations. The first of three planned trajectory correction burns was cancelled, as the spacecraft's flight path requires no fine-tuning at this stage. Any necessary adjustments can be incorporated into a subsequent correction burn.

ESA's European Service Module plays a critical role in this mission, providing propulsion, electrical power, thermal control, and the air and water needed by the crew.

## Upcoming Milestones

- **April 6**: Lunar flyby
- **~10-day mission end**: Pacific Ocean splashdown
