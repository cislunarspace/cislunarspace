# Blue Team Research

## Scope

This module publishes **open-source** compilations and analysis on blue team research. Sources must be lawful and publicly citable; no classified content.

## Mission and goals

Blue team research establishes a **systematic, updatable** understanding of adversary space operations based on open information. Analytical work shall align with how national space combat power is generated and employed, including formation paths, employment logic, and comparable technical and organizational factors.

Work synthesizes public doctrine, strategy, equipment, and employment patterns to support topic decomposition, research planning, and milestone assessment.

### Goal layers

| Layer | Scope |
|-------|--------|
| Strategic vision | Long-term human presence and use of space |
| National space | Sustained contribution to civil and security space capabilities |
| Force development | Cognitive, methodological, and technical support to force generation |

Blue team research provides an **intelligence and awareness foundation** alongside related engineering research.

## Research tracks

| Track | Subject |
|-------|---------|
| Doctrine & strategy | Public strategy, doctrine, and policy-to-action links |
| Equipment & technology | Architectures, programs, commercial participation (open sources) |
| Operations | Public cases, exercises, or bounded scenarios (e.g. orbital confrontation, kill-chain segments) |

Topics must stay **bounded**; overscoped questions should be split so sources remain tractable.

## Phased work

| Phase | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Literature & index | Verifiable bibliography, terms, and extracts |
| Thematic depth | Structured treatment within one track or cross-cutting issue |
| Publication | Citable text, figures, and revision history on this site |
